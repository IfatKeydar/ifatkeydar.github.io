The Electric Genome

A Mechanistic Hypothesis for Nuclear Architecture and Gene Expression Bias

Ifat Keydar
Independent Scientist, Tel Aviv, Israel

About

The Electric Genome is a scientific framework proposing that electrostatic forces guide the 3D arrangement of DNA in the cell nucleus. This spatial organization affects which genes turn on or off, offering new insight into genome regulation and disease.

This website accompanies my BioEssays hypothesis paper: The Electric Genome: A Mechanistic Hypothesis for Nuclear Architecture and Gene Expression Bias.
